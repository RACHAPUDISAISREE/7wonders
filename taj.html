TAJ MAHAL




The Taj Mahal, a renowned masterpiece of Mughal architecture, stands as an immortal symbol of love and devotion in Agra, India. Commissioned by the fifth Mughal emperor, Shah Jahan, in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal, the construction of this iconic ivory-white marble mausoleum began in 1632 and was completed in 1653.
Situated on the banks of the Yamuna River, the Taj Mahal is celebrated for its symmetrical beauty and intricate details. The central structure is flanked by four minarets, and its main dome is adorned with a stunning finial. The facade features intricate marble inlay work, showcasing a harmonious blend of Persian, Islamic, and Indian architectural styles.
The Taj Mahal's ethereal beauty is particularly enchanting during sunrise and sunset when the changing hues of the sky create a magical backdrop. Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Taj Mahal continues to captivate millions of visitors from around the world, offering a timeless tribute to eternal love.
